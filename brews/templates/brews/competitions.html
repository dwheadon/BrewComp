Welcome to BrewComp

Comparisons are hard and using scales that require finer distinctions than we can reliably make (such as the 20 point scale for flavor on the BJCP scorecard) introduces subjectivity and is much more prone to error (Guskey, Thomas R. "Chapter 1" What We Know about Grading, 2019). BrewComp attempts to eliminate these problems by making the levels of comparison smaller by breaking a large number of entries into size-limited heats and reducing the scale to a simple ranking (best to worst). The hope is that this will produce a much more accurate determination of the relative ranking of the brews in a smaller competition but could also be mapped back to the more traditional scale used by the BJCP to give a more absolute determination of how the brews would have scored.

BrewComp was designed and implemented by Dan Wheadon for the competitions held by the Chicago Home Brewer's (Facebook) Group . If successful, it could be made available to others who would like to use it for their own local competitions. Imlemented using Django, the source code can be found on GitHub. If you encounter any problems on the site please send feedback so we can continue to improve the product.
